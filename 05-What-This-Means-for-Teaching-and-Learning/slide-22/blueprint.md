# Slide 22 Blueprint

## Purpose

Put the most immediate classroom concerns in front of the audience in a compact, discussion-ready form.

## Core Takeaway

AI changes both the amount of thinking students can delegate and the trustworthiness of some traditional assignment products.

## Include

- A title combining student thinking and assessment.
- A guiding question such as `What thinking do we still need students to do?`
- Short bullets covering:
  - support vs. substitution,
  - cognitive offloading,
  - polished products vs. evidence of learning,
  - stronger alternatives such as process, explanation, revision, oral defense, and live performance.

## Visual / Layout Direction

- A split layout or grouped bullet layout would work well.
- Keep this readable and fast to scan.

## Speaker Guidance

- Emphasize that the issue is not simply cheating.
- Frame the real question as what kinds of AI use preserve meaningful learning.

## Transition

Broaden the conversation from student work to teacher practice and institutional responsibility.
