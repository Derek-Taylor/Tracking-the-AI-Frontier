# Slide 12 Blueprint

## Purpose

Turn the pace-of-progress story into an educational warning about stale assumptions, weak policy, and outdated assignment design.

## Core Takeaway

If educators are still operating from assumptions formed in 2023 or 2024, they may be underestimating both the promise and the disruption of current AI systems.

## Include

- The slide title.
- Three assumptions to challenge during later demos:
  - `AI can only do shallow work`
  - `AI outputs are always obviously bad`
  - `Students will only use AI for cheating`
- A concluding line that reinforces the institutional lag between technology change and school response.

## Visual / Layout Direction

- Keep this slide minimal and direct.
- Strong typography and spacing are more important than decorative visuals.
- The challenged assumptions should be immediately scannable.

## Speaker Guidance

- Connect outdated assumptions to policy, curriculum, and assessment decisions.
- Make clear that the problem is not misunderstanding one product, but misunderstanding the trajectory of the whole landscape.
- Use this slide to prepare the audience for the concrete demonstrations that follow.

## Transition

Close by setting up the next section: concrete frontier implementations that matter to educators.
